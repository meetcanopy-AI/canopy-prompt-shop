why_it_matters.txt

the thesis

The new internet belongs to people who can make things.

You do not need to become a software engineer. You do need to know how to direct tools, shape ideas, make assets, publish experiments, and build small useful things that belong to you.

Templates are not enough.

You need to know how to change the thing, not just fill in blanks.

Posting is not enough.

You need artifacts, systems, offers, and places people can click.

Prompts are not the product.

The product is the page, loop, app, guide, offer, or workflow you ship.

faq.txt

fear removal department

Questions before you enter?

Do I need to code?

No traditional coding background required. Some builds may touch code, but the recipes are written for beginners and focus on what to make, where to click, and what to change.

Do I need expensive tools?

No. Canopy starts with cheap/free paths first. Paid tools are optional upgrades when they actually help.

Is this just prompts?

No. Prompts are part of it, but the point is making real outputs: pages, visuals, apps, systems, offers, and digital products.

What if I’m not technical?

Perfect. Canopy is built for smart creative women who are tired of being locked out of technical spaces.
